Problem of the Week (042)

Find the sum of all positive integers $n$ such that the rational expression $$\frac{n^2 + 2n + 3}{n + 1}$$ yields an integer value.
Proof. We begin by applying polynomial division to separate the expression: $$\frac{n^2 + 2n + 3}{n + 1} = (n + 1) + \frac{2}{n + 1}$$ Since $(n+1)$ is an integer, the fractional term $\frac{2}{n + 1}$ must evaluate to an integer. Therefore, $(n + 1)$ must be a positive divisor of $2$. Solving this restricts $n$ to $1$. $\blacksquare$
